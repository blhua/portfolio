Biological Data Scientist

I am a data scientist, computational biologist, and research biologist with over 14 years of experience in molecular biology, microbiology, and synthetic biology. The cell is full of life secrets hidden away in troves of data. I am passionate about building tools to make sense of the complex data of the cell and to apply these fundamental learnings to drive advancement in healthcare and build a greener world.

From engineering bacteria, sustainably manufacturing key biomolecules in agriculture, to developing the next generation of vaccines to combat the most pressing infectious diseases, data science and computational biology lie at the core of our generation's greatest potential advancements.

Projects

Genome-wide analysis of RNA secondary structure in mammalian gene expression

mRNA structure and stability can markedly affect protein expression. This project compared mRNA secondary structure near every start codon with protein expression levels in HEK-293 cells to uncover new guidelines for the optimal design of RNA-based therapeutics for humans.

SARS-CoV-2 variant tracking

SARS-CoV-2 variants are constantly evolving, and it is essential that we adapt our vaccines to address new variants of concern. This tool interacts with the CDC COVID-19 database to identify changes to the predominant SARS-CoV-2 variant in the US and draws relevant sequence information of these variants.

Blog Post Source Code
Identifying conserved epitopes for vaccine design

The immune system plays a crucial role in overcoming infections and preventing future disease. Recently, vaccine strategies have focused on training the immune system to recognize conserved epitopes of proteins of infectious agents, potentially providing better breadth of coverage from future infection. This tools programatically identifies conserved epitopes of proteins of interest for use in vaccine design.
